# Physics 2: Physical Science & Technology (PHYC10004)

## Course Description

[Physics 2: Physical Science & Technology, University of Melbourne Handbook](https://handbook.unimelb.edu.au/2026/subjects/phyc10004)

This subject is designed for students with a sound background in physics whose interests lie in applications of physics to systems in the physical sciences, technology, and engineering. It introduces calculus techniques to support the study of core physical principles and real-world applications.

Topics include:

- **Fluids:** pressure in fluids, buoyancy, fluid flow, viscosity, and surface tension, with applications such as breathing, hydraulics, and flight.
- **Electricity and magnetism:** electric charge and fields, circuits, capacitance, resistance, magnetic fields, Faraday’s law, Maxwell’s equations, and electromagnetic waves, with applications in devices, motors, power systems, and communications.
- **Quantum and atomic physics:** photons, blackbody radiation, matter waves, quantisation in atoms, spectroscopy, lasers, and x-rays.
- **Nuclear physics and radiation:** atomic nuclei, radioactive decay, half-life, ionising radiation, fission, fusion, radiation safety, and applications such as carbon dating and stellar atom formation.
