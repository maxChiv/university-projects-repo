1. Project Summary

Project Name: Research Pricing and Costing Tool (RPCT)

Client: Melbourne University - Research, Innovation and Commercialisation (RIC)

Summary:

The current RPCT is an Excel-based workbook used at the University of Melbourne for budgeting research projects. While functional, it suffers from:

  • Instability across platforms.

  • Confusion between “costing” and “pricing.”

  • Undue complexity requiring extensive help resources.

  • Lack of integrated approval mechanisms, resulting in budget documents being emailed for sign-off with a bunch of people (ie finance, RIC, etc).

  • Contains multiple tabs, dense formulas, and unclear fields.

  • Requires manual copying/pasting of signatures for approvals.

The proposed solution is a web-based RPCT to assist researchers and administrators in costing and pricing projects in accordance with the Research Funding, Costing and Pricing Policy. It will:

  • Automate calculations for direct and indirect costs.

  • Support pricing adjustments and comparison against cost.

  • Provide an integrated approval workflow with notifications and status tracking.

  • Maintain centralised access to approved budgets for authorised users.

This tool is intended for internal University use only, but outputs can populate grant applications, determine scope of work for a defined budget, or provide a price to a funder (when appropriate).

2. Objectives

The system will:

  1. Allow projects to be fully costed, including salary and non-salary sections.

  2. Enable pricing adjustments (margin, discounts) to determine final project price.

  3. Summarise project cost vs. price and University position in a clear report format.

  4. Include an approval workflow with:

    • Automatic notifications and reminders.

    • Ability for approvers to review, comment, or request changes.

  5. Deliver a final approved budget accessible to authorised admin users.

  6. Must function on modern browsers (Chrome, Edge, Safari) across desktop.

3. Target Users

Primary: Grant Applicants, RIC admin.

Secondary: Faculty supervisors, finance team.

Other Stakeholders:

  • Primary Decision Makers: Project lead, Faculty Heads, RIC leadership.

  • Consulted Parties: IT department, finance department, faculty supervisors, PhD representatives.

4. Key Features / Functions

  • Input research parameters (e.g., project duration, resources, staff time, consumables). ✱

  • Automated costing calculations using internal university rates. ✱

  • Pricing adjustment options with immediate recalculation. ✱

  • Export functionality to PDF✱ and Excel formats.

  • Approval workflow with notification system (assumed just emails for simplicity).

  • Web-based, user-friendly interface accessible on major browsers.

✱ AS SEEN IN EXCEL WORKBOOK

5. Scope

In-scope:

  • Internal research project costing and pricing.

  • Automated application of UoM salary rates and indirect cost rates.

  • Internal approval process with digital tracking and notificiations.

  • Report generation in PDF/Excel.

  • Inline help, tooltips, examples, and context panes; glossary for costing terms

Out-of-scope:

  • External funding body budget templates (e.g., ARC/NHMRC-specific tools).

  • Compliance checks with relevant policies and funding rules.

Unsure:

  • Integration with UoM authentication systems (SSO).

  • Communication channels with the application process:

    • eg: RIC Admin → Candidate (using comments in the system) RIC Admin → Other departments (some form of chat)

6. Constraints / Assumptions

  • Must comply with UoM data storage, retention, and privacy policies…?

  • Must use UoM Single Sign-On for authentication.

  • Data must be securely stored on internal UoM hosting.

  • Approval workflows must be supportive of cross-faculty inquiries

  • Must integrate with existing University databases and linked services.
