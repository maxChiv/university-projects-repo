Sprint 1 Review

Date: 08/09 12pm
Facilitator: Eugene
Note Taker: Max

All members were present.

Purpose

Share what was delivered and get feedback from stakeholders.

Sprint Goal Review

Goal: Improve and refine the budget costing and pricing tool design to better align with stakeholder expectations and enable a smoother approval flow.
Did we achieve it? Yes the sprint focused on design updates based on Frank’s feedback and additional feature refinements.

Completed Work

Design Updates & Feedback Integration

Story / Task: Incorporate Frank’s design feedback into hi-fi model improvements
Outcome / Demo:

  • “Other Costs” simplified into Staff and Non-Staff categories

  • Created separate Cost Summary and Pricing pages

  • Added flexibility for discounts (lump sum or %) and margins on individual cost items (admin-only controls)

  • Ensured in-kind contributions are excluded from pricing

  • Confirmed alignment with UniMelb tools for design inspiration → positive feedback received

Stakeholder Feedback:

  • Grouping categories into Staff/Non-Staff is clearer

  • Strong preference for Cost Summary vs Pricing distinction

  • Confirmation that direction is aligned with UniMelb standards

Related Jira: Improvements & Fixes for Hi-fi Model

New Features / Changes

Approval Flow

  • Approvers now receive email notification + PDF view of project

  • Users can add additional viewers (e.g., supervisors, RIC staff)

  • If project is edited post-approval: approval emails are resent and clear staging process introduced

Budget & Email Export Enhancements

  • Added CC option for multiple viewers in export form

  • Ensured only one approver, but multiple viewers allowed

Summary Page Improvements

  • All costs displayed with prompt: “How will you modify this?”

  • Clear descriptions added for improved clarity

  • Cost recovery multiplier (1.5) applied to total project cost (visible only to admin)

Stakeholder Feedback:

  • Positive reception of the approval flow, especially resending approvals when edits are made

  • CC option was requested and addressed

  • Desire for clarity on what message approvers see when budget changes → flagged for refinement

Not Completed Work

  • Some clarifications still needed around approver messaging when budgets change (to be addressed in next sprint).

Key Learnings

  • Design clarity is critical – simplifying categories and separating summaries significantly improved stakeholder confidence

  • Approval flows are sensitive areas – ensuring correct permissions, staging, and notifications reduces risk of confusion

  • Importance of admin vs end-user roles in handling discounts, margins, and cost recovery

Stakeholder Notes / Feedback

  • Praise for clearer design direction and alignment with UniMelb tools

  • Requests for more explicit messaging around budget changes for approvers

  • Suggestion to further test PDF export formatting before rollout

Next Steps

  • Implement design refinements in line with feedback to finalise hi-fi model for approval

  • Address remaining messaging clarity for approvers

  • Continue stakeholder testing of approval and export workflows
