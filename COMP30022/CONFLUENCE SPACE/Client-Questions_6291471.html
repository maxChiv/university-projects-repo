Group Twenty Two Project : Client Questions

Created by Jay Schellens, last modified by Max Chivers on Aug 15, 2025

  • Does the client want us to integrate into the university’s current infrastructure?

Prompting Questions

  • What is the core problem we're solving?

    • e.g. "The current costing and pricing Excel tool works but isn't user-friendly."

  • Who are the end users? (e.g. researchers, admin staff, finance approvers)

  • What is the main goal of our web interface?

    • Better usability? More transparency in cost breakdown? Quicker approvals?

  • What are the core features of the Excel tool?

    • Cost input, auto-calculations, approvals, validations, export, etc.

  • What are its strengths? (e.g. completeness, compliance)

  • What are its pain points?

    • Usability, complexity, onboarding, accessibility

  • Ask: Can we get access to the workbook, documentation, and e-learning module?

    • (in-training website module)

  • Who will use the system (personas)?

    • Researchers (casual, experienced), research administrators, finance teams

  • Who are your stakeholders?

    • Supervisors, research office, finance teams, IT, ethics approval bodies?

  • What kind of internal approvals are required?

  • What does success look like?

    • Working prototype, usability improvements, functional parity with Excel?

  • What should be in scope vs out of scope?

  • Do we need to match all Excel features or just the most important ones?

    • can we remove unnecessary features?

  • Any tech stack requirements? (e.g. university-mandated frameworks)

  • Will the system be used online, offline, or both?

  • What existing systems do you already use and should we take inspiration from these?

  • What makes a UI more user-friendly in this context?

    • Simplified flows, guided steps, context-sensitive help, error prevention

  • Do we want to keep Excel-like structure or rethink it entirely?

  • Should we support progressive disclosure (show advanced features only when needed)?

  • What roles do we each want to take?

    • e.g. project manager, UX designer, backend/frontend dev, documentation

  • How will we communicate? (e.g. Slack, Teams, Notion)

  • How will we track progress? (e.g. Trello, Jira, shared doc)

  • Are we calculating the expected grant approval price after calculating the cost of the project?

  • How do you want the approval system to work? Export in a summary as a pdf or same interface that we login and then have a separate page to view?
