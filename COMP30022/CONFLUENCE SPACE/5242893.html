Meeting Notes Summary – RPCT Discussion

This meeting mainly covered short introductions,

Introductions

  • Frank (Director, Research Grants, Contracts & Finance) introduced his role and gave context:

    • Current Excel tool has been used for 500+ schemes because it’s highly flexible.

    • Backend rules (e.g., salary/indirect rates) can be updated easily.

    • Wants to separate cost vs price more clearly.

    • Approval routing should be built into the tool (doesn’t need signatures, just an in-tool process).

    • Final tool outcome doesn’t need to be as complex as the Excel workbook.

    • Admins should have ability to modify backend rules/rates.

  • Trish (Manager, International Research Grants) introduced — currently fielding RCPT queries.

Problems with Current Excel RCPT

  • Too complex (large workbook, many tabs, redundant forms).

  • Confuses costing and pricing (done simultaneously, unclear).

  • No approval process built in → staff don’t know who to email.

  • Affiliate researchers don’t always fit into system.

  • Redundancy (duplicate forms, internal vs external summaries).

Desired Features / Needs

  • Separation of Cost vs Price

    • Costing: salaries, non-salary costs, on-costs/overheads.

    • Pricing: margin/discount applied afterwards.

  • Approval workflow

    • Simple routing within system (could be emails recorded internally).

    • No signatures required.

    • Timestamp of approval for audit.

  • Outputs

    • Internal: Price vs Cost summary (for researcher + admins).

    • External: Budget form/quote (for funders).

    • Final approved budget accessible by multiple people.

  • Interface

    • Helpful contextual information on pages (tooltips, guidance).

    • Stable, web-based, simpler than Excel.

  • Flexibility

    • Admins can update salary scales, rates, rules easily.

    • Retain ability to adjust margins/discounts.

Not Strictly Necessary (Nice-to-Haves)

  • PSDA tab – not needed.

  • Internal sharing form – not needed.

  • On-cost calculator – complex, nice-to-have, not critical.

  • Multi-currency support – optional, can be simplified to manual input.

  • Overly detailed breakdowns (keep it simpler).

Benefits to Retain

  • Flexibility to adapt to many funding schemes.

  • Easy updates to salary/indirect cost rates.

  • Covers both salary and non-salary cost components.

Next Steps

  • Email from Eugene to set up recurring meetings with the client.

    • Consider setting up meetings at the end of each sprint for progress updates.

  • Focus on solving the highlighted main problems, not replicating Excel’s complexity.

  • Demonstrate feasibility that UniMelb can build/own such a tool internally.
