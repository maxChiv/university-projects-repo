# Real Analysis (MAST20026)

## Course Description

[Real Analysis, University of Melbourne Handbook](https://handbook.unimelb.edu.au/2026/subjects/mast20026)

This subject introduces mathematical analysis through both a careful theoretical framework and selected applications. A strong emphasis is placed on rigorous proof techniques, including mathematical induction and proof by contradiction.

Core topics include:

- the distinction between real and rational numbers,
- convergence and divergence of sequences (including the Cauchy criterion),
- infinite series and standard convergence/divergence tests,
- continuity and differentiability of single-variable functions,
- key results such as the Mean Value Theorem and Taylor’s theorem,
- the Riemann integral and rigorous proof of the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus,
- power series and Taylor series representations of elementary functions, and
- Fourier series for periodic functions.
